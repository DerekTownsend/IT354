Microservices vs. Monolith

Microservices applications spit the work into smaller teams and APIs. These APIs then handle some of the logistics for you. Several teams will be working on these so that the code and its effect on the business is well understood. The benefit to this system is that there should be no parts of the code that no one understands. Also the system is easier to scale and maintain.

The main problem I have with this is that I feel like relying on multiple APIs can lead to incompatibilities popping up and restricting future changes or growth. Also I feel like this is more tailored to larger companies and not to small start ups. If you manage and maintain the applications yourself then you can cut out the middleman and possibly save the company money in the long term because as your needs grow the more resources you are giving to another company to manage something you can probably do yourself after a certain point.

Monolith applications are built withe the database, frontend, and backend all maintained by the company but as it grows become a bit of a tangled mess. But the trade off seems to be more direct control over all aspects of your code
